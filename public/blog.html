Understanding Fire And It's Causes

YATHARTH‚Äù Fire safety concerns all those provisions and activities taken to avert as far as possible fire accidents, lessen the damages from such accidents, and guarantee safe escape during emergencies. Fire safety includes the general fire prevention, detection, and response strategies. Examples of factors relevant to fire safety concern the installation of systems such as smoke detectors, fire extinguishers, and sprinkler systems; good electrical wiring; and safe handling of combustible materials.

A fire can be described as the outcome of a chemical reaction that includes rapid oxidation of fuel together with the use of heat and oxygen, generating flames, heat, and gases. Fuel, heat and oxygen (Fire triangle) needs to be set up for fire and without any of these existing simultaneously within the environment enables a fire to be extinguished.
